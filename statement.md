1. Problem Statement

Many students have a time finding the right schools and getting financial help. This is because:

There is no one place to find all the information: You have to look at lots of government and private websites to find scholarship and admission information.

It takes a time to do everything by hand: Comparing your grades and other information to what each school requires is time-consuming and easy to get wrong.

Students miss out on opportunities: Sometimes students do not even know about government grants or special college programs because they do not have enough information.

2. Project Objective

The main goal is to make a computer program that helps students figure out which colleges and government scholarships they're eligible for. Students can put in their grades and personal information. The program will give them instant feedback to help them make decisions. The program is like a helper that makes the process easier.

3. Target Users

High School Students: These are students who want to know how to get into college.

Scholarship Applicants: These are students from backgrounds who need financial help.

Parents: These are the people who help their kids plan for college and careers.

4. Core Features

A. College Eligibility Features

Checking the age requirement: The program checks if you are old enough to apply to a school.

Checking your grades: The program checks if your grades are good enough to get into a school.

Evaluating entrance exams: The program checks if your entrance exam scores are good enough to get into a school.

B. Scholarship Eligibility Features

help for girls and non-binary students: The program knows about special government programs for these students.

Considering sports achievements: The program looks at sports certificates to see if you are eligible for scholarships.

Evaluating requirements at once: The program can handle complex situations where you need to meet many requirements at the same time.

5. User Interface (UI) Design

The program is designed to be easy to use so:

It has a menu: You can easily navigate to different parts of the program.

It asks questions: The program asks you simple questions to get the information it needs.

It shows results in a way: The program shows you a simple report that says which schools and scholarships you are eligible, for.

It is friendly: The program is designed to be welcoming and easy to use for students and parents.
